Step 1 of 6

Prep the Machine

Need help?

Why flush first?It warms the group and portafilter so the coffee doesn't hit cold metal — cold gear makes sour shots.

How long to warm up?Wait until the ready light is steady. A few extra minutes never hurts.

Need help?

How fine is "fine"?Like table salt, almost powdery — much finer than for a French press. If unsure, leave the grinder where it is; it's already dialled in.

Overflowing or half empty?Level it off with a finger and re-weigh. 17–19 g is fine.

Step 3 of 6

Tamp

Need help?

How hard do I press?Firm handshake pressure. Level matters far more than force.

Is it level?Look at the tamper from the side — it should sit flat, not tilted.

Need help?

Too fast (under 20 s)?The water raced through. Next shot: grind finer, or check you used the full 18 g.

Too slow (over 35 s)?Next shot: grind coarser, or tamp a little lighter.

Tastes sour?Under-extracted — grind finer or let the shot run a touch longer.

Tastes bitter?Over-extracted — grind coarser or stop the shot a touch earlier.

This shot is "wrong"?Drink it anyway if it's close — adjustments are for the next one.

Step 5 of 6

Steam the Milk

Need help?

Skipping milk?Making a straight espresso or americano? Swipe on past this step.

Big bubbles?Keep the wand tip just under the surface at the start, then deeper. Tap the jug on the counter and swirl to smooth it out.

Why wipe right away?Milk bakes onto a hot wand in seconds and is a pain to clean later.
